MDB Growth Capital
Prepared for Gareth Kobrin, Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer — Yonda Tax

350 clients, a $15 million raise, and 60% of your customers in the United States. The pipeline engine hasn't matched any of that.

The product wins every evaluation it enters. The gap is that most US evaluators aren't finding Yonda in the first place.

The Core Leak

The product wins. Businesses that use Yonda cut their tax overhead dramatically, onboard in days rather than months, and pay a fraction of what Avalara charges. The proof exists. It is just not in market.

Two case studies are published for 350 clients. The $15 million raise closes the resource argument — the capital is there to build the engine. The question is whether it gets built fast enough to capture the US market before Avalara alternatives and Fonoa's funded content machine claim the evaluation traffic first.

Avalara charges $19,000 median annually, has opaque pricing, and persistent support complaints. Every unhappy Avalara customer searching for an alternative is a warm prospect for Yonda. Most of them do not find Yonda first.

The gap is not product. The gap is the system that connects Yonda's proof to the buyers who need it.
What This Seems to Indicate

The proof programme is 99% untapped

One existing case study produced five distinct proof points from a single client interview — cost savings, checkout cost reduction, accuracy rate, monthly time overhead, and states automated. Yonda has 350 clients. The content library that should be driving paid media does not exist at the scale the client base supports.

US market penetration is running on a product most US evaluators haven't heard of

60% of clients are in the United States but there is no paid media programme, no comparison content for US-based alternatives, and no proof specifically framed for US ecommerce operations. The buyers are there. The discovery mechanism is not.

Avalara switcher traffic is at a high and Yonda isn't capturing it

Avalara's move to private equity ownership drove a wave of customer dissatisfaction. Search volume for "Avalara alternative" has risen substantially. Yonda has a strong case for that audience — faster onboarding, lower cost, better support — but no dedicated comparison page to intercept that traffic.

The creative production gap is structural, not a resourcing problem

Building a content and creative engine by hiring serialises the problem — research, hire, ramp, then build. At 100% year-on-year growth with investor expectations attached to it, that timeline does not fit the window. The engine needs to be running, not being assembled.

What MDB Runs First
1

Run the proof programme across the existing client base

Structured client interviews using a framework that extracts quantifiable outcomes — cost reduction percentages, time saved per month, states automated, accuracy rates. Turned into case studies, paid media assets, and testimonial content formatted for LinkedIn, paid search, and outbound email. MDB runs this as an ongoing programme — not a one-time exercise.

Produces: A growing library of proof assets that compound in paid media and organic search over time.
2

Launch comparison pages for US evaluation traffic

Dedicated comparison pages for Yonda versus Avalara, Yonda versus TaxJar, and Yonda versus Vertex. Built around the specific concerns US ecommerce operators have — pricing transparency, onboarding speed, support quality, and integration depth. Optimised for the search terms Avalara switchers actually use.

Produces: Evergreen acquisition pages capturing high-intent evaluation traffic Yonda is currently losing.
3

Run a paid media programme for the US ecommerce segment

Paid LinkedIn and Meta campaigns targeting US ecommerce finance leads and operations directors. Anchored on the proof library — specific outcomes, quantified, in the first line. Not brand awareness. Evaluation-stage content that reaches buyers in the window before they shortlist.

Produces: A running US demand gen motion that generates pipeline from the 60% market Yonda already serves.
4

Build the Avalara alternatives comparison page

A single high-production page capturing "best Avalara alternative" and "switch from Avalara" search traffic. Verified proof points. Fast onboarding claim backed by documented case data. Pricing comparison using Yonda's fixed model against Avalara's opaque enterprise pricing. The highest-return single page Yonda can publish.

Produces: The primary organic acquisition asset for the largest warm prospect pool in Yonda's market.
The Shift

Right now, Yonda grows through word of mouth, inbound from existing clients, and the product's reputation on G2. That got the company to 350 clients and a $15 million raise. It will not sustain the growth rate a post-raise investor expects. MDB is the engine that runs the US go-to-market motion. The proof programme generates content continuously. The comparison pages capture evaluation traffic permanently. The paid media campaigns reach the US ecommerce segment on a running basis. Gareth and the Yonda team run the product and the client relationships. MDB runs the pipeline.

Which feels like the bigger gap right now — the proof library that isn't being captured from existing clients, or the US evaluation traffic that isn't finding Yonda?
